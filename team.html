Meet the Founders

Bohdan Khomtchouk, Ph.D.
Co-founder and Chief Quilting Officer
Stanford postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Biology (Bio)

Highest speed hardest core hacker around -- 30KLOC annually, on the toughest stuff: novel bioinformatics algorithms and analysis, new API development, language bindings, interface design, clustered computing, distributed-computation language implementations, etc. Dr. Khomtchouk brings >10 years experience in bioinformatics and computational biology research. He is the 2018 winner of the NIH/NIA Stanford Training Program in Aging Research grant, author of two CRC Press textbooks and over half a dozen peer-reviewed publications, including a PhD in computational human genetics and genomcics, and lead developer of the world's fastest heatmap software (shinyheatmap). Responsibilities at Bioquilt include:

  • People: Team building, team lead, tech lead, technology directions, process and QA directions.
  • Core infrastructure work of Bioquilt, including all the patented quilting algorithms and architecture (forthcoming), UX/UI design, performance/efficiency optimizations, and open-source community outreach efforts.

Evan Kaeding
Co-founder and CEO

Evan has spent the past 5 years building data products and data-based marketing channels for clients and internal stakeholders. With a rare ability to speak fluently with technical operators and with business users, he helps bridge the gap between opportunity and execution. As a former VP of Business Development at Billy and current Director of Marketing and Analytics at Cooladata, Evan advises new customers on analytics implementations for marketing and product usage. Working with both business and technical stakeholders, he helps craft solutions that deliver transformational business value.

Or Gozani, M.D., Ph.D.
Co-founder and Chairman of the Board

Dr. Gozani is the Morris Herzstein Endowed Chair Professor at Stanford University. Dr. Gozani received his B.A. at U.C. Berkeley and his M.D and Ph.D. degrees from Harvard Medical School. Dr. Gozani also did his postdoctoral training at Harvard Medical School in the lab of Dr. Junying Yuan. Dr. Gozani’s lab focuses on understanding how chromatin-signaling networks regulate key physiologic and pathologic programs. For his work in chromatin biology and epigenetics, Dr. Gozani has received numerous honors including a Burroughs Wellcome Career Award in Biomedical Sciences, a Kimmel Scholar Award, a Searle Scholar Award, and an Ellison Senior Scholar in Aging Award. Dr. Gozani is also co-founder of Epicypher (bio-reagent company) and Athelas Therapeutics (anti-fungal pharma company). He has served in multiple leading advisory roles such as the Scientific Advisory Board of GlaxoSmithKline.
