Meet the Founders

Bohdan Khomtchouk, Ph.D.
Co-founder & Chief Quilting Officer (CQO)
Stanford postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Biology (Stanford profile)

Highest speed hardest core hacker around -- 30KLOC annually, on the toughest stuff: novel bioinformatics algorithms and analysis, new API development, language bindings, interface design, clustered computing, distributed-computation language implementations, etc. Dr. Khomtchouk brings >10 years experience in bioinformatics and computational biology research. He is the 2018 winner of the NIH Stanford Training Program in Aging Research grant from the National Institute on Aging, author of two CRC Press textbooks and over half a dozen peer-reviewed publications, including a PhD in computational human genetics and genomics, and inventor of the world's fastest heatmap software (shinyheatmap). Responsibilities at Bioquilt include:

  • People: Team building, team lead, tech lead, technology directions, process and QA directions.
  • Core infrastructure work of Bioquilt, including design and implementation of algorithms and architecture, UX/UI engineering, performance/efficiency optimizations, and open-source community outreach efforts.

Meet the Scientific Advisory Board

Dario Ghersi, MD, PhD
Nicholas Tsinoremas, PhD

Dr. Dario Ghersi is an Assistant Professor of Biomedical Informatics at the School of Interdisciplinary Informatics at the University of Nebraska at Omaha (UNO). His teaching interests include bioinformatics, computational biology, and genomics. He received his M.D. from the University of Genoa, Italy and his Ph.D. in Computational Biology at New York University. Prior to starting his faculty career at UNO, Dr. Ghersi was a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Mona Singh's lab at Princeton University. His creative and research interests include cancer genomics, computational immunology, structural bioinformatics of protein binding, and protein interaction networks.

Dr. Nicholas Tsinoremas holds faculty appointments at the Department of Medicine and Health Care Informatics, University of Miami Miller School of Medicine and the Computer Science Department, College of Arts and Sciences at the University of Miami (UM) and is the Founding Director for the University of Miami Center for Computational Science. He also serves as Director of Biomedical Informatics for the Miami CTSI. He received his B.A. in Chemistry from the University of Athens, Greece, and his Ph.D. in Molecular Biology from the University of Leeds, UK. Before joining the University of Miami, he served as Senior Director of Informatics at The Scripps Research Institute, Florida. There, he recruited and managed the Informatics and IT groups and established the translational informatics/high-throughput screening integrated platform for the newly formed Scripps Florida. As Director of Computational Genomics and Genomic Discovery at Rosetta/Merck Co., he directed the project that combined informatics and computational approaches with gene expression profiling and genetics to discover, prioritize, and define drug target genes and biomarkers. Prior to working for Merck/Rosetta, Tsinoremas was the Vice President of Genomics at DoubleTwist Inc., where he determined the scientific direction of DoubleTwist’s bioinformatics applications and databases.
