Meet the Founders

Bohdan Khomtchouk, Ph.D.
Co-founder
Stanford University, Department of Biology & Department of Medicine

Dr. Khomtchouk is an American Heart Association (AHA) Postdoctoral Fellow working in the field of computational epigenetics and cardiovascular medicine in the Gozani & Assimes Labs at Stanford University. He brings >10 years of experience in bioinformatics and computational biology research to Quiltomics. He was the 2017-2018 recipient of the prestigious Stanford Training Program in Aging Research (TPAR) grant from the National Institute on Aging (NIA) and the 2014-2017 recipient of the National Defense Science and Engineering (NDSEG) Graduate Fellowship from the Department of Defense (DoD). Dr. Khomtchouk has co-authored two CRC Press (Taylor & Francis) textbooks and over half a dozen peer-reviewed publications. He holds a PhD in Computational Human Genetics and Genomics from the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine, as well as three Bachelor's Degrees summa cum laude in Mathematics, Physics, and Molecular Biology/Biochemistry. Responsibilities at Quiltomics include:

  • People: Team building, team lead, tech lead, and technology directions.
  • Core infrastructure work of Quiltomics, including design and implementation of novel algorithms and computational architecture, UX/UI engineering, performance/efficiency optimizations, and open-source community outreach efforts.


Evan Kaeding
Co-founder & CEO

Evan has spent the past 5 years building data products and data-based marketing channels for clients and internal stakeholders. With a rare ability to speak fluently with technical operators and with business users, he helps bridge the gap between opportunity and execution. As a former VP of Business Development at Billy and Director of Marketing and Analytics at Cooladata, Evan brings a wealth of experience advising new customers on analytics implementations for marketing and product usage. Working with both business and technical stakeholders, Evan helps craft solutions that deliver transformational business value. Currently, Evan serves as a member of the Media department at Wieden+Kennedy, where he helps clients derive value from the gargantuan amount of data generated by online advertising.


Or Gozani, M.D., Ph.D.
Co-founder
Stanford University, Department of Biology

Dr. Gozani is the Morris Herzstein Endowed Chair Professor at Stanford University. Dr. Gozani received his B.A. at U.C. Berkeley and his M.D and Ph.D. degrees from Harvard Medical School. Dr. Gozani also did his postdoctoral training at Harvard Medical School in the lab of Dr. Junying Yuan. Dr. Gozani’s lab focuses on understanding how chromatin-signaling networks regulate key physiologic and pathologic programs. For his work in chromatin biology and epigenetics, Dr. Gozani has received numerous honors including a Burroughs Wellcome Career Award in Biomedical Sciences, a Kimmel Scholar Award, a Searle Scholar Award, and an Ellison Senior Scholar in Aging Award. Dr. Gozani is also co-founder of two privately-held biotech companies (EpiCypher, Inc. and Athelas, Inc.). He has served in multiple leading advisory roles such as the Scientific Advisory Board of GlaxoSmithKline.

Meet the Scientific Advisory Board

Dario Ghersi, MD, PhD
Nicholas Tsinoremas, PhD

Dr. Dario Ghersi is an Assistant Professor of Biomedical Informatics at the School of Interdisciplinary Informatics at the University of Nebraska at Omaha (UNO). His research interests include bioinformatics, computational biology, and genomics. He received his M.D. from the University of Genoa, Italy and his Ph.D. in Computational Biology at New York University. Prior to starting his faculty career at UNO, Dr. Ghersi was a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Mona Singh's lab at Princeton University. His research work involves cancer genomics, computational immunology, structural bioinformatics of protein binding, and protein interaction networks.

Dr. Nicholas Tsinoremas holds faculty appointments at the Department of Medicine and Health Care Informatics, University of Miami Miller School of Medicine and the Computer Science Department, College of Arts and Sciences at the University of Miami (UM) and is the Founding Director for the University of Miami Center for Computational Science. He also serves as Director of Biomedical Informatics for the Miami CTSI. He received his B.A. in Chemistry from the University of Athens, Greece, and his Ph.D. in Molecular Biology from the University of Leeds, UK. Before joining the University of Miami, he served as Senior Director of Informatics at The Scripps Research Institute, Florida. There, he recruited and managed the Informatics and IT groups and established the translational informatics/high-throughput screening integrated platform for the newly formed Scripps Florida. As Director of Computational Genomics and Genomic Discovery at Rosetta/Merck Co., he directed the project that combined informatics and computational approaches with gene expression profiling and genetics to discover, prioritize, and define drug target genes and biomarkers. Prior to working for Merck/Rosetta, Tsinoremas was the Vice President of Genomics at DoubleTwist Inc., where he determined the scientific direction of DoubleTwist’s bioinformatics applications and databases.
